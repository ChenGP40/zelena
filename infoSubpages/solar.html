Solar Energy
What is Solar Energy?
Solar energy technology is split up into two main groups: photovoltaics (PV) and concentrating solar-thermal power (CSP). PV, used in solar panels, is the most common form of solar energy technology. Energy from sunlight is absorbed by the solar panels, creating electrical charges that are converted into electricity. The solar panels are connected to either the grid or a battery to allow the energy produced to be used in your home. CSP technology is used mostly in large power plants, but small-scale versions can be used in residential areas. CSP uses mirrors to concentrate energy from sunlight onto receivers, where it is converted into heat that can be used to generate electricity.
Prerequisites:

There are multiple solar power options available for different environments and budgets. To determine which is best for you, you can first utilize online mapping services and tools to evaluate your home, but it is best to hire a professional solar installer who can provide recommendations and estimates before you make any commitments. To install solar panels on your property, it must have good solar potential: access to sunlight and little shade from trees and other structures. If you determine that your home has a good solar potential, installing solar panels will likely bring you the most benefits. If you are a tenant or your home is not suitable for solar panels, there may be a community solar program near you that you can become involved with.

Costs:

Purchasing your own solar energy system typically costs between $14,321 and $21,960, depending on its size and efficiency. Installation costs must also be considered, so you should call companies in your area to ask for quotes. If you are unable to purchase or take out a loan for your own solar energy system, you can lease, make a PPA, or take part in a local Solarize program. Leasing a solar energy system allows you to pay a flat monthly fee to use a third party’s solar panels installed on your home. This is less of a commitment than purchasing a solar energy system, but it reaps the same benefits. The cost of leasing a solar energy system will vary depending on how much the third party in your area will charge. PPAs or Power Purchase Agreements are similar to solar leases, but you host solar panels from a solar company installed by a developer with little or no installation costs. Solarize programs are formed by a group of homeowners and business owners who will buy solar energy equipment in bulk to lower their prices. This allows more people in the community to access solar energy without as much of a financial commitment.

If you are unable to install a solar energy system on your property, you can take advantage of a community solar program. These programs make solar power available to low-income homeowners and businesses that would otherwise be unable to access it. They often utilize off-site solar panels connected to a grid; community members can subscribe and pay to receive a portion of the electricity generated by their solar project. More community solar projects are being created throughout the country, and some projects are created specifically for low-income communities. To find community solar projects near you, you can contact your electricity provider or search for opportunities online.

Financial Incentives:

If you have a solar energy system installed on your property, you will save about $10,000 to $50,000 on electric bills over 25 years. Additionally, solar panels will add about $15,000 to the value of your home. You can also purchase a storage battery that will allow you to use your solar power in any weather conditions and sell any excess electricity your system produces for more profit. The IRS offers Residential Clean Energy Credit, a 30% tax credit for the cost of new clean energy property, decreasing the cost of your solar energy system. These benefits apply to you if you purchased or utilized a Solarize program to obtain a solar energy system for your home. If you have solar panels on your property by leasing or making a PPA, you will still save between $10,000 and $50,000 on electricity bills and can sell your excess energy, but the solar panels will not add value to your property and you do not qualify for Residential Clean Energy Credit. With community solar projects, you can choose how much of an investment you make, and your profits will reflect that. This can lower electricity costs for anyone, no matter their income. However, community solar projects do not apply for any federal tax credit.
