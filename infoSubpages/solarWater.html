Solar Water Heating
What is Solar Water Heating?
Most solar water heaters use concentrating solar-thermal power (CSP) technology, which can also be used to generate electricity in solar panels. These systems use mirrors to concentrate energy from sunlight onto receivers, where it is converted into heat. The heat is then transferred into water. Other systems rely solely on energy from the sun without any technology to amplify it. Solar water heating systems are either active, with circulating pumps, or passive, without circulating pumps. Active systems have either direct or indirect heating systems: direct systems circulate water through the collectors, while indirect systems circulate a non-freezing heat-transfer fluid through the collectors that will later heat the water. Passive systems are either integral collector-storage systems, which consist of a transparent tank that allows sunlight to get through, or thermosyphon systems, which heat water on the roof of a home before circulating it through the plumbing system.
Prerequisites:

Solar water heaters can be used in any climate, but the type of system you choose must support your area’s weather conditions. If you are looking for an active system, you should use a direct circulation system for areas where it rarely freezes and an indirect circulation system for an area with frequent freezing temperatures. Passive systems should only be used in warmer climates with rare freezing temperatures. If your system is mounted on a rooftop, you should ensure that your home’s roof is in good condition and can support the necessary mounting equipment.

Costs:

These systems can cost as little as $1,500 but could range up to $13,000 for advanced systems. Passive systems are typically less expensive and last longer than active systems, but they are less efficient. However, active systems typically require more maintenance. Installation costs will vary depending on the availability of contractors in your area and the complexity of your solar water heating system, so you should call local companies for quotes to get the most accurate estimate. You will still need to own a conventional water heater to use as a backup if there is no sunshine, but you will still benefit from having a solar water heater and using less fossil fuels.

Financial Incentives:

Solar water heating systems typically decrease monthly water heating bills by 50% to 80%. If your previous water heater used natural gas to heat your home’s water, you will also save money by using less fossil fuels. Solar water heaters qualify for the IRS’s Residential Clean Energy Credit, a 30% tax credit on all newly purchased and installed clean energy property. Any costs from purchasing a solar water heater will be decreased by 30% through a federal tax credit.
